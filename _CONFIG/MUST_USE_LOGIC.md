## CORE PRINCIPLES

### 1. Respect BOTH Market and Model

- **NEVER** underestimate the market — they usually know something
- **NEVER** dismiss the model — it has data we don't see
- Both have blind spots: Model lacks injury/news info; Market can overreact to narratives

### 2. When Market and Model Disagree

The disagreement is usually because:
- Market has **injury info** the model doesn't
- Market is **risk-managing** based on schedule/motivation
- Market is pricing **this specific match**, not averages

**Key Question:** Is the market soft/aggressive ENOUGH given the circumstances?

## MARKET VS MODEL GAP ANALYSIS

### When Market is SOFTER Than Model (Favorite Gets Worse Line)

| Scenario | Analysis | Action |
|----------|----------|--------|
| Minor injuries (1-2 players) | Market may be overreacting | Check if model value exists |
| Major injuries (3+ key players) | Market is correct to soften | Check if soft ENOUGH |
| AFCON/International duty | Significant impact | Verify market adjustment is sufficient |
| Fixture congestion | Rotation expected | Usually market is right |

**Example:** Man United vs Newcastle — Model said -0.25, market softened to 0.00 due to 7+ injuries. But 0.25 gap wasn't enough for 7 missing players. Market should have favored Newcastle. **Result: Market wasn't soft enough.**

### When Market is MORE AGGRESSIVE Than Model

| Favorite Location | Pattern | Action |
|-------------------|---------|--------|
| **Home favorite** | Often FAILS to cover | Lean SKIP or underdog |
| **Away favorite** | Market may know something | Investigate before deciding |

**Example:** Milan vs Verona — Market -1.50, Model -1.25. Market was MORE aggressive on home favorite. Usually this fails, but Milan had 9-game H2H dominance + Verona 0 wins in 38 San Siro visits. **Result: Market was right due to extreme H2H pattern.**

## COMPARABLE-MATCH PRICING (CMP)

### How to Calculate CMP Properly

1. **Find Team A vs similar-level opponents** (same venue, similar context)
2. **Find similar-level teams vs Team B** (same venue, similar context)
3. **Compare market prices** from those matches to current match
4. **Adjust for conditions:** injuries, red cards, match intensity, schedule

### Home vs Away Adjustment

**General rule:** Home advantage ≈ 0.50 goals

| If Team A vs Team B at A's home | Then Team B vs Team A at B's home |
|--------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| Team A -0.50 | Should be ~0.00 (pick'em) |
| Team A -1.00 | Should be ~Team B -0.50 |

*Assumes no injuries or significant form changes*

## LESSONS FROM POST-MATCH ANALYSIS

### 1. H2H/VENUE ADVANTAGE + MARKET CONFIDENCE = BET

H2H and venue patterns alone are NOT the signal. The signal is **whether market is pricing that advantage confidently**.

| Scenario | Market Behavior | Action |
|----------|-----------------|--------|
| H2H/venue advantage exists + Market AGREES (aggressive) | Market knows and is confident | **BET** |
| H2H/venue advantage exists + Market DISAGREES (soft/backs opponent) | Market knows something we don't | **CAUTION** |

**Example — Milan vs Verona:**
- H2H: Milan 9 straight wins, Verona 0 wins in 38 San Siro visits
- Model: -1.25 Milan
- Market: -1.50 Milan (MORE aggressive)
- **Market KNOWS the venue/H2H advantage and is CONFIDENT** → BET Milan
- Result: Milan 3-0 ✓

**Example — If Market Had Been Soft:**
- Same H2H/venue stats
- But if market was -0.75 or pick'em while model said -1.25
- This means market is NOT confident despite the advantage → investigate why

**Rule:** H2H/venue patterns are context. Market confidence in pricing that advantage is the signal.

### 2. WHEN MARKET BACKS OPPONENT DESPITE H2H/VENUE ADVANTAGE

If historical advantage exists for Team A but market is backing Team B:
- Market has information we don't
- Injuries, motivation, tactical changes may override history
- **DO NOT** blindly trust H2H — trust the market's skepticism

**Rule:** H2H/venue advantage + market backing opponent = SKIP or investigate deeply.

- This is the STRONGEST signal available

- Trust the pattern to repeat
- If market contradicts recent same-venue H2H = **bet against market**

**Example — Forest vs Everton (Dec 30):**
- Everton beat Forest 3-0 at City Ground just 3 weeks prior
- Market had Forest as -0.50 favorites

### 16. WHEN TO OVERRIDE MARKET SKEPTICISM

From Roma vs Genoa (Dec 29) and Newcastle vs Burnley (Dec 30):

| Condition | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| H2H EXTREME (15+ or 6+ consecutive) | Consider overriding market |
| Opponent has CRITICAL crisis (GK, 5+ out) | Strengthens override case |
| Market still backs favorite (even softly) | Pattern likely continues |

**Rule:** When to override market skepticism and BET the favorite:

All THREE must be true:
1. H2H dominance is **EXTREME** (15+ wins OR 6+ consecutive wins)
2. Opponent has **CRITICAL crisis** (GK crisis, key suspensions, 5+ absences)
3. Market still favors the dominant team (just at soft odds)

→ **BET the favorite. Market is likely overreacting to short-term issues.**

**Example — Roma vs Genoa (Dec 29):**
- Roma 16/18 home wins vs Genoa (35 years without Genoa win)
- Genoa had 3rd choice GK
- Market soft @ 2.22 on Roma -1.00
- We SKIPPED (trusted market skepticism)
- Result: Roma 3-1 — we missed +1.22u

**Example — Newcastle vs Burnley (Dec 30):**
- Newcastle 5 straight wins vs Burnley (6 years since Burnley PL win)
- Newcastle had 6 defenders out BUT H2H too strong
- Result: Newcastle 3-1 — pattern continued despite crisis

- Rotation + fatigue + AFCON = reduced attacking intensity

- **Lean HEAVY Under**

Newly promoted teams with elite home records (8+ unbeaten) maintain that form even with absences.

When the gap between Model and Market on Totals is 1+ goals, the market usually knows something the model doesn't.

**Example — Liverpool vs Leeds (Jan 1):**

- Model is pricing historical averages
- **TRUST MARKET'S CAUTION on Totals**

## RED FLAGS — WHEN TO STOP AND RECONSIDER

1. You're about to call "MODEL ERROR" — do you have overwhelming evidence?
2. You're betting on H2H/venue advantage but **market is backing the opponent** — why is market not confident?
3. You're fading market's aggressive line because of "short-term form" — does market know something?
4. You said SKIP but you're adding a "best bet" anyway — why?
5. You're backing a "crisis" team to lose — have you seen them adapt before?
6. Market and model AGREE but you want to go opposite — what do YOU know that they don't?
7. Team just had ONE good result but key player still absent — is form really "returning"?
8. You're backing Under but underdog just scored 4+ in recent game — recent form vs H2H pattern?
9. Same teams played at same venue 3-4 weeks ago — are you respecting that result?
